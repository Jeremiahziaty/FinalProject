Game Rules

The Objective:

The objective of the game is for the player to hold a set of cards that add up to 21 or get as close as they can without exceeding 21.
The only way to win is to have a better hand than the dealer.

Place the Bet:

Before the dealing of cards starts, the player will be asked how much he would like to bet.
The bet can range from two dollars up to five hundred dollars.

The Deal:

Two cards will be dealt to the player. A choice of “stand” or “hit me” is available after the deal.

Gain and Loss of Money:

If a blackjack is obtained by the player and the dealer does not have one, the dealer
immediately pays the player one and a half of the amount of the bet.
If both dealer and player have blackjack, the game ends in a tie and the player keeps what he betted.
If either the player or dealer exceed 21 at any time, the game results in a bust for the individual.


Terms of the Game:

“Stand” stay with the cards dealt
“Hit me” deal another card with the intention of getting closer to 21
“Blackjack” getting 21 on the first deal
“hit” each card dealt after the first two cards
“double” multiplying the current bet by two
“bust” cards totally up to over 21, resulting in a loss of the game and loss of money.

Card Values:

Cards marked 2-10 are valued at the face value
Ace is the special card and is either 1 or 11
King, Queen and Jack are all valued at 10
